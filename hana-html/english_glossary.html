Club Regime

Type of international regime with restricted membership and exclusive benefits for participants, as the JSI-EC tends to configure itself.

Complex of Regimes

Interconnected network of international institutions addressing related aspects of the same issue. In digital trade, it includes the WTO, regional agreements with e-commerce chapters, and other digital governance forums.

Co-Conveners

The three coordinating countries of JSI-EC negotiations: Australia, Japan, and Singapore. Responsible for facilitating "structured discussions" and announcing progress in the negotiation process.

Credibility of Commitments

Strategic motivation identified with the highest empirical alignment (36 codes). Involves sovereignty pooling for inter-temporal political commitment lock-in, differentiation by issue and country, and transaction cost reduction.

Cramer's V

Statistical association measure used to evaluate the strength of the relationship between categorical variables. EGOV presents Cramer's V = 0.725, indicating a very strong association with JSI-EC participation.

Defensive Standards

Preferences that tend to retain greater margins of state sovereignty in regulatory coordination processes.

Digital Government Development (EGOV)

Electronic government development index developed by UNDESA for 2020. Main explanatory variable of the model, with Cramer's V = 0.725. Countries with high EGOV participate at 100% in JSI-EC vs. 9.8% of countries with low EGOV.

Digital Trade

Cross-border electronic commerce encompassing all commercial transactions conducted through digital means, including both digital products and the digital component of traditional goods and services trade.

Digital Trade Network Density (DTND)

Institutional advantages indicator based on the number of Preferential Trade Agreements with e-commerce chapters (ecPTAs) signed by a country and notified to the WTO.

Electronic Transmissions

Broad definition contained in Article 11.1 of the JSI-EC "stabilized" Joint Declaration, signed in June 2024: "transmission made using any electromagnetic means and includes the content of the transmission".

Evolutionary Determinants

Factors related to institutional consolidation dynamics: tipping point thresholds (1/3 of universe), intensive temporality (singular vs. ordinary moments), and post-M2 consolidation that reduces the relative weight of material capabilities.

Federal Ideology

Tertiary strategic motivation (8 codes). Involves appropriate forum for all members, axiological rationality, and intrinsic values of multilateral coordination.

Global Governance

According to Drezner (2007): "not only the codified adjustment of national norms and regulations" but also "the set of authority relationships designated to oversee, enforce, and modify any transnational set of norms and regulations".

Harmonized Regime

Type of international regime characterized by uniform norms applicable to all participants, such as the WPEC tariff moratorium.

Institutional Advantages

Benefits derived from prior participation in commercial agreement networks. Operationalized in two indicators: trade network density (TND) and digital trade network density (DTND).

Institutional Inflection

Point of institutional consolidation based on Finnemore and Sikkink (1998). The JSI-EC reaches this point in January 2019 (M2) when China joins, marking the transition from 71 to 77 members and establishing conditions for venue consolidation.

Integrative Model

Hybrid approach combining factors of material capabilities and institutional advantages to explain state preferences. Achieves 87.3% predictive accuracy in statistical analysis.

JSI-EC-WTO

Joint Statement Initiative on Electronic Commerce of the World Trade Organization. Plurilateral venue created in 2019 with 91 participating countries out of 164 WTO members to establish future binding rules on electronic commerce.

Material Capabilities

Set of structural resources possessed by a State that influence its regulatory coordination preferences. Operationalized in three indicators: digital government development (EGOV), secure server density (SSD), and national income (NI).

Moravcsik's 3-Step Methodology

Adaptation of Moravcsik's (1998) sequential model analyzing: Step 1 (Substantive Cooperation Preferences), Step 2 (Institutional Choice Preferences), and Step 3 (Negotiating Core Preferences).

National Income (NI)

World Bank classification for 2020 based on GNI per capita, operationalized in four ordinal categories (high, upper middle, lower middle, low) and subsequently dichotomized (higher/lower).

National Ideology

Anti-JSI-EC strategic motivation (8 codes). Involves claiming WPEC as the only legitimate track and rejecting the plurilateral venue for eroding national sovereignty.

Offensive Standards

Preferences that tend to favor formulas involving certain delegation or pooling of state sovereignty to obtain positive externalities.

Organizational Ecology

Theoretical approach emphasizing "the influence of institutional environments, especially their organizational density and resource availability, on organizational behavior and viability" (Abbott, Green, and Keohane, 2016). Adapted to analyze JSI-EC vs. WPEC dynamics in the WTO.

Preference Distribution

Frequencies observed in the study population at a specific historical moment regarding state preferences for regulatory coordination. The thesis identifies three key distributions: M1 (2017), M2 (2019), and M3 (2019-2024).

Preference Structure

Ordered spectrum of foreign policy options considering as baseline the state disposition to cede or retain sovereignty margins in exchange for potential positive externalities. Distinguished between defensive standards and offensive standards.

Preferences for Institutional Choice

Step 2: State preferences about where to negotiate (plurilateral JSI-EC vs. multilateral WPEC) and the preferred institutional design for regulatory coordination.

Preferences for Negotiating Core

Step 3: State preferences about what to negotiate specifically and on what terms of maximum and minimum predisposition to sovereignty pooling or delegation.

Preferences for Substantive Cooperation

Step 1: State preferences on whether to negotiate digital trade regulations NOW (participating in JSI-EC) or later (remaining only in WPEC).

Regulatory Coordination

Process by which States adjust their national regulatory frameworks to achieve coherence and compatibility with international standards, involving various degrees of sovereignty cession or retention.

Secure Server Density (SSD)

Number of secure servers per million inhabitants, taken from the "penetration" index developed by Netcraft for 2019. Proxy indicator of material capabilities related to digital infrastructure.

Sovereignty Pooling

State sovereignty grouping strategy where countries maintain joint control over certain regulatory aspects without completely delegating them to a supranational authority.

Stabilized Agreement

First normative package on electronic commerce agreed among 91 countries of the JSI-EC-WTO in July 2024. Includes a dozen points: online consumer protection, electronic signatures, spam, open government data, electronic contracts, transparency, paperless trade, cybersecurity, open Internet access, electronic transaction frameworks, electronic invoicing, and "single windows".

State Preferences

Ordering of foreign policy options based on the evaluation of expected costs and benefits of different international regulatory coordination strategies.

Structural Determinants

Material capabilities (EGOV, server density, national income) and institutional advantages (trade network density, digital trade network density) that influence tropisms toward coordination venues.

Tariff Moratorium

Postponement established since 1998 in the WTO WPEC for imposing tariffs on electronic transmissions. Represents the only multilateral agreement in force on electronic commerce.

Technocratic Centralized Management

Secondary strategic motivation (11 codes). Involves informational efficiency, "effective continuation of work," differentiation only by issue, and instrumental rationality.

Tipping Point

January 2019 (M2): Moment of JSI-EC institutional consolidation marked by China's incorporation, transforming the negotiating dynamic and establishing conditions for the plurilateral venue.

Trade Network Density (TND)

Institutional advantages indicator based on the number of Preferential Trade Agreements (PTAs) signed by a country and notified to the WTO.

Venue

Specific institutional space within an international organization where negotiations and regulatory coordination processes develop. The thesis distinguishes between the multilateral venue (WPEC) and the plurilateral venue (JSI-EC) within the WTO.

Venue Explicit Binding Norms

Legally binding rules emerging from international negotiation processes. The July 2024 stabilized agreement marks the JSI-EC transition from a sovereignty pooling strategy toward binding norms.

WPEC

Work Programme on Electronic Commerce of the WTO, established in 1998. Original multilateral venue that includes all 164 WTO members and maintains the tariff moratorium.
